Pilot Rationale

The first build should be easy to evaluate inside the business.

The pilot should give office staff, technicians, and management a shared way to judge whether the workflow is faster, clearer, and easier to support before expanding scope.

ScopeOne service workflow

Due work, scheduling, field entry, review, and PDF reporting are enough to test the operating value.

TestingReal devices

DecisionClear next step

Workflow Fit

The iOS pilot should follow the exact loop Weeks already described.

1Due work

Month-based recurring service list by area, call type, skip, hold, and seasonal timing.

2Office schedule

Weekly board assigns jobs, dates, and technicians while referencing Sage work order numbers.

3Technician visit

My Day view, site notes, equipment, checklist, readings, samples, photos, repairs, and follow-up.

4Office review

Office approves the visit, cleans report language, and protects access notes from customer output.

5Customer report

Branded PDF report gets sent with or alongside the Sage invoice.

Pilot Inputs

Weeks stays in control of the accounts, testers, and operating data.

Apple access

Weeks owns or creates the Apple Developer/App Store Connect account. 57 can be added with the right build/developer access.

Pilot team

Pick 1-2 office users and 2-3 technicians who can test the service loop and give direct feedback.

Staged data first

Start with sample or sanitized accounts, tasks, and photos. Access notes stay internal and out of customer reports.

Weekly decisions

A single Weeks owner keeps scope moving and confirms what should be changed, deferred, or accepted.

Success Criteria

A successful pilot answers the management questions that matter.

Field use

Can a technician complete assigned visits, notes, readings, photos, and follow-up items without paper?

Office use

Can office staff schedule, review, approve, and prepare a customer report without duplicating work?

Customer output

Does the report look professional enough to support the customer relationship and Sage invoice process?

Next decision

After 30 days, Weeks chooses whether to harden, expand, pause, or change direction based on actual use.

TestFlight builds are for beta testing and are time-limited, so the stabilization period includes a recommendation for the next build or production path.

Scope Discipline

These are intentionally not in the first pilot unless Weeks chooses them.

DeferredDeep Sage integration

The pilot references Sage work order numbers. Automated Sage sync should wait until the workflow proves itself.

DeferredOn-site invoicing or payments

Useful later, but not required to solve scheduling, service logging, review, and reporting.

Scope decisionFull offline mode

Basic autosave/retry is included. A robust offline queue can be added if field connectivity demands it.

Scope decisionAutomated customer emailing

The pilot can generate reports for office sending. Automated sending can be added after report language is settled.
